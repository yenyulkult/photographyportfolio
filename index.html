Artist Statement

Photography, for me, is a meditation on presence. Each frame is an invitation to pause and witness the extraordinary hiding within the ordinary. I seek the interplay of shadow and light, the quiet stories etched in weathered faces, and the fleeting poetry of urban landscapes at twilight.

My work spans street photography, intimate portraits, and architectural studies—each genre offering a different lens through which to explore human experience and our relationship with the spaces we inhabit.

Portraits

Portrait 1 of 2

Portrait

Story & Rationale:This portrait captures a young woman seated casually on a staircase in a bright, modern interior, portraying a relaxed yet confident moment. The subject was photographed in a natural, unstaged pose to reflect her genuine personality and sense of ease rather than a formal or posed appearance. The clean architectural lines of the space were used to frame the subject simply, while soft natural light from the surrounding windows provides even illumination and a calm, airy atmosphere. The low camera angle adds subtle presence and strength to the composition, and overall, the image aims to convey a sense of quiet confidence, authenticity, and individuality.
